Background

What is Solitreo?

Solitreo is the cursive script historically used by Sephardic Jews to write Ladino (Judeo-Spanish). Unlike the block Hebrew script used for sacred texts, Solitreo was the everyday handwriting of Ladino speakers across the Ottoman Empire, North Africa, and beyond.

Ganchos Sans is a digital typeface designed by Benny Yorohan that revives these letterforms, translating the fluid curves of Solitreo into a contemporary typographic system. This tool translates names into generative woven patterns inspired by Sephardic textile traditions.

Heritage

Letters Become Textiles

Throughout the Sephardic diaspora, textile arts — particularly rug weaving — were central to domestic life. This tool connects two traditions: the written Ladino script and the woven patterns of Sephardic textiles.

Each name entered is first transliterated from the Latin alphabet into Solitreo characters. The shapes of those characters are then deconstructed — their density, symmetry, and stroke directions — to generate a unique binary weaving pattern compatible with AdaCAD and traditional loom techniques.

Method

How It Works

1. Transliterate — Your name is converted from Latin script to Solitreo using Ladino phonetic rules based on the Aki Yerushalayim standard.

2. Analyze — Each Solitreo letter is rasterized and its visual properties are extracted: fill density, center of mass, symmetry, stroke direction, and structural complexity.

3. Generate — These properties become parameters for a generative algorithm that composes a rug pattern: borders, field zones, medallions, and symmetry — all derived from the unique geometry of your name's letters.

4. Weave — Export the pattern as a WIF file for AdaCAD or as a PNG image. Every name produces a one-of-a-kind design.
